The chart as a living tool

There's a quote I keep coming back to. It's from Herbal Alchemy by Phillip Hurley:

"The key to the secrets of alchemy lies in the language and science of astrology."

Not many people realize this, but the base language of alchemy — the whole tradition of transformation, of turning one thing into another, of working with elemental forces — is written in astrological terms. The planets aren't just symbols on a wheel. They're active forces. And people have been working with those forces for centuries.

Everything you've learned in this course so far has been about reading. Observing. Understanding the energies at play in your solar return year. This module is about the next step: working with those energies intentionally. Not just knowing what's in the atmosphere — but actively using it.

The chart is already telling you which planetary energies are most alive for you right now. Here's how to meet them where they are.

The planetary week

You already know this system — you just don't know you know it. The days of the week are named for the planets. This isn't coincidence. It's a remnant of a time when planetary timing was woven into daily life.

Sunday ☉ Sun
Monday ☽ Moon
Tuesday ♂ Mars
Wednesday ☿ Mercury
Thursday ♃ Jupiter
Friday ♀ Venus
Saturday ♄ Saturn

Each day of the week carries that planet's energy. When you start something on a Thursday — Jupiter's day — you're initiating under an energy of expansion and abundance. Friday is Venus's day: good for creative work, connection, things of beauty. Tuesday is Mars's day: good for taking action, confronting something that needs to be confronted, getting things done. Saturday is Saturn's day: good for structure, discipline, laying groundwork.

This isn't superstition. It's timing. And timing is one of the most underused tools in any practice.

The planetary hour

It goes deeper than the day. Each day is also divided into planetary hours — every hour has its own ruler, cycling through all seven classical planets in a specific order. The first hour of the day always belongs to that day's ruling planet. From there, the cycle continues.

This means you can get incredibly specific with your timing. You don't just choose the right day — you choose the right hour within that day for what you're trying to do.

Example — timing for expanded communication

Say you want to start something aimed at expanding your reach — a new project, a pitch, a creative endeavor that involves getting your ideas out into the world.

You'd start on a Thursday (Jupiter's day — expansion, abundance, growth) during the hour of Mercury (the planet of communication, ideas, and connection). Jupiter gives it room to grow. Mercury gives it a voice.

That's astrological magic. Two layers of timing, working together intentionally.

The planets and the body

For as long as people have been reading the stars, they've also been mapping them onto the human body. Each planet corresponds to specific physical systems — organs, glands, structures that carry that same quality of energy.

This is one of the foundations of medical astrology and herbal healing traditions. It's not a replacement for medical care — please work with your doctors and practitioners. But as a layer of awareness, it's remarkable. When a planet is strongly activated in your solar return, the body system it rules may need extra attention, support, or nourishment that year.

☽Moon Alimentary canal, female reproductive organs, lymph glands, cerebellum, sympathetic nervous system

♃Jupiter Posterior pituitary gland, liver and blood plasma
♄Saturn Parts of the adrenal glands, bones, teeth, joints, tendons, spleen
♅Uranus Parathyroid gland, brain and cerebrospinal system
♆Neptune Pineal gland
♇Pluto Pancreas, digestive glands, sexual organs

Look at your solar return chart. Which planets are most active this year — sitting in angular houses, making strong aspects, conjunct your Sun or Moon? The body systems those planets rule are worth paying attention to. Not with fear — with care. Nourish what the year is working hard.

The plants

Throughout this course, you've been introduced to herbal allies for each sign and planet — plants that carry the same energetic frequency and can support you in understanding your year.

This is a different layer. These plants are specifically for working with planetary energy — for use in intention-setting, ritual, and the kind of active magical practice that turns knowing into doing. Each planet has a botanical tradition connected to it. These are the plants you reach for when you want to consciously align with a planetary force.

If Jupiter is your year's dominant energy, you keep sage on your altar. If you're doing work during Mercury's hour on a Wednesday, you brew rosemary tea first. If your solar return has a prominent Mars and you want to work with that fire constructively, you use mugwort or nettles in your practice.

How to work with these plants

You don't need a formal ritual. You need an intention. Brew the plant as a tea, burn it as incense, keep it on your desk, add it to your food, hold it on your altar — whatever form feels accessible and real to you. The method matters less than the intention behind it. You're working with the planet's energy. The plant is your bridge.

These are correspondences, not prescriptions. They are not medical advice. Use what resonates. Leave what doesn't.

You may use any of these plants with the intention of harnessing that planet's energy to aid in your endeavors. Welcome to witchcraft, baby.

Putting it all together

Here's the thing about your solar return: it doesn't just tell you what the year holds. It tells you what energy is most available to you. And energy you're aware of is energy you can work with.

If Jupiter is prominent in your chart this year, you start things on Thursdays. You reach for sage, olive, or walnut. You tend to your liver. You set intentions during Jupiter's hour. You don't fight the expansion — you consciously direct it.

If Saturn is sitting heavy in an important house, you work on Saturdays. You use yarrow and wheat. You pay attention to your bones and joints. You do the slow, structural work that Saturn rewards — and you do it in Saturn's timing, because Saturn respects people who show up prepared.

The chart is showing you the weather. Planetary magic is knowing how to dress for it — and how to make the most of whatever's in the forecast.
