Before we start: four things you need

Casting a solar return chart is easier than it sounds. You don't need any software — just a browser, a free account on one website, and four pieces of information.

The first

Your birth date

The day, month, and year you were born. This one's easy.

The second

Your birth time

As exact as possible. The most reliable source is your birth certificate. If you don't have that, a parent's memory, a baby book, or hospital records can work. Even a rough hour is better than nothing.

The third

Your birth city

The city or town where you were born — not where you grew up, where you were actually born.

The fourth

Your birthday location

Where you'll be — or where you were — on your birthday this year. This one surprises people. We'll talk about why it matters in a moment.

If you don't know your birth time: use noon as a placeholder. The chart will still generate, and some of it will be genuinely useful — the signs your planets are in (SR Sun sign, SR Moon sign, and all other planetary sign placements) will be reliable, and the aspects between planets will mostly hold. What won't be reliable: your SR Rising sign and all house placements. Both depend entirely on an accurate birth time, so if you're working from noon, treat anything involving the Rising sign or which house a planet falls in as approximate. Focus on the planetary signs and aspects — there's real information there. An unknown birth time is not a reason to skip this course. Know what to lean on and what to hold loosely.

Why your birthday location matters

Remember from Module 01: your solar return chart is cast for the moment the sun returns to its exact natal position. That moment in time is fixed — it happens when it happens.

But where you are on earth when it happens? That changes the chart.

Because the chart is a snapshot of the sky from a specific location. Change the location, change the chart. The planets will be in the same signs, but the houses — the areas of life they fall into — will shift depending on where you're standing when the sun comes home.

The solar return chart is cast for where you are on your birthday, not where you were born.

So if you spend your birthday in your hometown, use your hometown. If you're traveling, use wherever you'll be. If your birthday has already passed this year and you stayed home, use home.

Some people intentionally travel on their birthday to shift the chart — to move a planet into a more favorable house, or out of a difficult one. That's a real technique called solar return relocation. It's fascinating and we won't go deep on it here, but I want you to know it exists so you understand why the location field on astro.com is not optional.

The tool we're using: astro.com

There are several free chart websites, and most of them will generate a solar return. I use astro.com. It's the industry standard for professional astrologers — not the prettiest interface, but it's accurate, it's free, and it's been around long enough that it's not going anywhere.

You can use it as a guest without making an account. But if you create a free account, it saves your birth data so you don't have to re-enter it every time. That's worth the two minutes it takes to sign up.

Okay. Let's go pull your chart.

The walkthrough

Follow these steps in order. The astro.com interface can look overwhelming at first, but you only need to touch a few things.

  1. Go to astro.com. Type it in directly: astro.com. You're looking for the Astrodienst homepage.
  2. Find "Free Horoscopes" in the top navigation and hover over it. From the dropdown, click "Extended Chart Selection." If you're on a mobile device, the nav may collapse into a menu icon — tap that first, then look for Extended Chart Selection.
  3. Enter your birth data. You'll see a form with fields for name, date, time, and place of birth. Fill everything in. For the city field, start typing and use the autocomplete — make sure you select the right city from the list rather than just leaving the typed text. If you have a saved account, your birth data may already be filled in. Just verify it looks correct.
  4. Scroll down to the chart options. Look for a dropdown or section labeled "Chart type" or "Method." Click it and select "Solar Return."
  5. Set the year. After you select Solar Return, a field for the year should appear. Choose the year you want to cast for. If your birthday hasn't happened yet this calendar year, use the current year. If your birthday has already passed, use next year — that's your upcoming solar return.
  6. Set your solar return location. This is the "where you'll be on your birthday" field. Enter that city — not your birth city. Use the autocomplete to select the correct match. This field is easy to overlook. Make sure it's not still showing your birth location before you generate the chart.
  7. Click the button to generate the chart. It's usually orange, labeled something like "Click here to show the chart." Go ahead and click it.

Your chart will load. It's going to look like a circle divided into sections, with symbols scattered throughout and lines crossing through the middle. That's exactly what it should look like. You did it.

What you're looking at

Let's do a quick orientation before we dive in.

The large circle is your chart wheel. It's divided into twelve sections — those are the houses, the areas of life. The symbols placed within those sections are the planets and points. The lines running through the center of the wheel are the aspects — the relationships between planets that we talked about in Module 01.

Along the outer edge of the wheel, you'll see small glyphs marking where one sign ends and another begins. The symbols inside each wedge show which planets have landed in that slice of the chart.

It's a lot to take in all at once. And you don't need to read all of it today.

We're going to move through this chart piece by piece. You only need to find three things right now.

Your three landmarks

Everything else we do in this course is going to orbit around these three. Find them on your chart and just notice where they are. You don't need to interpret anything yet.

↑

Look for "AC" or "ASC"

SR Rising Sign

On the left side of the chart wheel, look for a horizontal line extending outward — that's the Ascendant axis. The letters "AC" or "ASC" mark it. The sign sitting right there is your SR Rising sign. It sets the tone for the whole year ahead.

☉

Look for the circle with a dot

SR Sun

The Sun glyph is a circle with a dot in the center — ☉. Find it on the chart. Note what section (house) it's sitting in, and what sign it's in. The SR Sun is the most important placement in the whole chart. It tells you where your core energy is going this year.

☽

Look for the crescent

SR Moon

The Moon is a crescent — ☽. Find it and note its house and sign. The SR Moon points to your area of emotional focus and growth for the year. It's where the work is, and where the evolution is.

Got those three? AC/ASC for your Rising, ☉ for your Sun, ☽ for your Moon. Write them down — sign and house number for each. That's your starting material.

A note on house systems: This course uses whole sign houses — the simplest and oldest house system, and the one that makes the most sense for beginners. In whole sign houses, whichever sign is rising becomes the entire 1st house. The next sign in order is the entire 2nd house. The sign after that is the 3rd house — and so on, all the way around the wheel. Every house is exactly one sign. This means once you know your rising sign, you can calculate any planet's house by counting signs from the ascendant. We'll use this throughout the course. (Astro.com defaults to Placidus houses — if you want to confirm a placement using whole signs, you can change the house system in the chart settings under "Extended Chart Selection.")

The Sun's herbal allies

This module is all about the Sun — its return, its homecoming, the moment the whole chart gets born. So it feels right to introduce the Sun's plant allies here.

The Sun corresponds to warmth, vitality, radiance, and core self-expression. The plants associated with the Sun carry those same qualities.

🌿

Sun herbs — your energetic allies for this work

Ash tree, angelica, almond, bay tree, burnet, butterbur, chamomile, frankincense. As you begin working with your solar return chart — casting it, sitting with it, getting curious about it — these are friends you can keep close. A cup of chamomile while you study. Frankincense burning while you read. A bay leaf on your altar as an anchor. Use what resonates. Leave the rest.

These are correspondences — energetic resonances, not prescriptions. Work with them however feels true to you, or don't work with them at all. The chart is the potion. The herbs are just good company while you brew it.
