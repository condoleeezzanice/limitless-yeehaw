Welcome to Module 04. This is where the real reading begins.

We looked at the SR Rising in Module 03 — the frame, the tone, the costume the year is wearing. Now we go inside the frame. The SR Sun and SR Moon are the two most important placements in your solar return chart. Between them, they tell you where your energy is going this year and where your growth is happening. They're the backbone of everything. If you only ever understand these two, you have what you need.

We'll take them one at a time. Starting with the Sun.

---

## ☉ THE SR SUN

### What the SR Sun tells you

The Sun is the most important planet in the solar return chart — which makes sense, because the whole chart is cast for the moment the Sun comes home. In your solar return, the Sun tells you where and how you're likely to focus your basic energy over the next twelve months. What you're pouring yourself into. What becomes the central preoccupation of the year.

The Sun's house placement is primary — it tells you *where* the energy is going. The Sun's sign tells you *how* it's being expressed. We'll look at both. But the house is the one to spend time with.

Wherever the SR Sun lands, that house becomes the arena of your year. That's where the action is. That's where you're being asked to show up.

Find the Sun glyph on your chart — the circle with a dot in the center, ☉ — and note which house it's sitting in.

---

### SR Sun through the 12 houses

**Sun in the 1st house.**

The Sun is front and center — literally. This is a year of heightened self-focus, personal reinvention, and energy directed squarely at you: how you're showing up, what you're becoming, how you want to be seen.

It can feel self-centered, because it is — in the best way. This is a year to invest in yourself first. A new chapter is beginning and you are the main character of it.

---

**Sun in the 2nd house.**

Your energy goes into building — money, resources, material foundations, and the deeper question underneath all of it: what you actually value and what you believe you're worth.

Income, what you own, financial stability — these become central concerns. But so does self-worth. The 2nd house is where we find out what we think we deserve. This is a year to get honest about both.

---

**Sun in the 3rd house.**

The Sun lights up communication, ideas, and your immediate world. Writing, speaking, teaching, studying — these become your primary outlets. Siblings, neighbors, short trips, and the constant movement of information through your daily life all carry extra energy this year.

This is a year for staying curious and putting your thoughts into the world. The ideas are there. The work is giving them a voice.

---

**Sun in the 4th house.**

The year turns inward and domestic. Your energy goes into home, family, and the private foundations of your life. You might move, renovate, deepen family relationships, or do the emotional work of understanding where you came from and what you're still carrying from it.

The outside world takes a back seat this year. That's not a setback — it's an invitation to tend to what's underneath everything else.

---

**Sun in the 5th house.**

One of the most enlivening placements. The Sun in the 5th lights up creativity, self-expression, romance, play, and the things you do just because they make you feel alive. Children — literal or metaphorical creative projects — may be part of this year's story.

This is a year to make things and enjoy yourself without apologizing for it. Joy is not a detour. This year, it's the point.

---

**Sun in the 6th house.**

Your energy goes into the daily — the systems and habits that hold your life together, the quality of your work, the state of your body. Health becomes a priority, by circumstance or by choice.

It's not a glamorous placement but it's an important one. This is a year where getting your foundations right — physically, practically, in your daily work — pays off in ways that compound for years afterward.

---

**Sun in the 7th house.**

Partnership is the focus. One-on-one relationships — romantic, professional, significant — take center stage this year. What you're building with another person, or the person you're looking for. Contracts, commitments, important collaborations.

This is a year where who you're doing life with matters enormously. The mirror of relationship is where you'll learn the most about yourself.

---

**Sun in the 8th house.**

One of the more intense placements — the Sun in the 8th asks you to go deep. Transformation, other people's resources, mental health, shared finances, power dynamics, and the places where your life is entangled with someone else's all come into focus.

This is not a surface year. What gets examined tends to change you in lasting ways. The depth is where the medicine is.

---

**Sun in the 9th house.**

The Sun in the 9th wants to go further — through travel, through study, through rethinking your entire belief system. Higher education, publishing, philosophy, international experiences, and the big questions about what you actually believe and why.

This is a year of broadening — what you know, where you go, what you think is possible. The horizon is closer than it looks. Move toward it.

---

**Sun in the 10th house.**

The Sun in the 10th puts you in the spotlight professionally. This is a year where your work is seen, where your reputation is being made or solidified, where ambition is not only appropriate — it's the assignment.

What do you want to be known for? This is the year to do the work that earns that.

---

**Sun in the 11th house.**

The Sun lights up your social world and your sense of purpose within something larger than yourself. Groups, friendships, networks, the causes you care about, the vision of what could be — all energized this year.

This is a year to find your people and build something with them. What you hope for, who you hope for it with — these are the year's central questions.

---

**Sun in the 12th house.**

The Sun in the 12th is one of the most misunderstood placements. It's a year of retreat, inner work, and invisible preparation — things happening behind the scenes, in the unconscious, in solitude. It can feel like the world has gone quiet around you.

That's not a loss. It's a gestation. Something is preparing to emerge — and the most important work you can do this year is the kind no one else can see yet. Trust the quiet. It's doing something.

---

### A note on the SR Sun sign

The house your SR Sun occupies is the primary reading. But the sign it's in adds texture — it tells you how that energy is moving.

Quick example: Sun in the 10th house in Cancer versus Sun in the 10th house in Aries. Both years are about career and public life. But the Cancer version brings nurturing, emotional intelligence, and a quietly tenacious quality to that arena. The Aries version is bolder, faster, more willing to go first and fight for recognition. Same house, different flavors.

Take your SR Sun's sign and layer it onto the house reading. The house is the where. The sign is the how. Together, they give you the full picture.

## ☽ THE SR MOON

### What the SR Moon tells you

The SR Moon is often described as your emotional needs for the year — but that undersells it. The Moon in your solar return indicates an area of emotional intensity, challenge, and purposeful evolution. It's not just where you feel things. It's where you grow.

There's always progress from year to year with the SR Moon. This year's placement builds on where the Moon was last year and points toward where it's going next. You're not starting over each time — you're continuing a thread. The Moon is the part of you that's always becoming something.

The SR Moon is an area of emotional focus in your life over the next year — and a point of possible growth. It's where the work is. And where the evolution is.

Find the crescent — ☽ — on your chart. Note the house. Then find it below.

---

### SR Moon through the 12 houses

**Moon in the 1st house.**

The emotional work centers on you — your identity, your image, how you feel about yourself. Mood is visible. Others will sense your inner state before you've said a word.

The growth is in letting that be okay — in learning that emotional transparency is not a weakness. Who you're becoming emotionally is becoming visible this year, whether you planned for that or not.

---

**Moon in the 2nd house.**

Money and self-worth carry more emotional charge than usual. Security — what you have, what you fear losing, what you feel you deserve — becomes a live emotional question. The growth is in understanding your relationship to worth: where you learned it, and whether it's still true.

Financial fluctuations are possible. So is a meaningful shift in how you value yourself.

---

**Moon in the 3rd house.**

Emotionally, the mind is busy. Communication becomes a site of feeling — what you say, how you say it, what goes unsaid. Sibling relationships or your immediate community may carry emotional texture. The growth is in learning to speak from feeling rather than around it.

Journaling, talking it through, writing — these become important emotional outlets this year.

---

**Moon in the 4th house.**

The Moon is at home in the 4th — it's the house she naturally rules — which makes this placement powerful and tender in equal measure. Family, home, roots, and patterns handed down through your lineage are all emotionally activated. Old things surface. Things you thought you'd already processed.

The growth is in the foundations. What are you healing at the root level?

---

**Moon in the 5th house.**

The emotional focus lands on creativity, romance, and what makes you feel alive. Your heart is more open this year — and more exposed. Creative expression becomes a meaningful emotional outlet. Love and children — literal or creative — bring both delight and vulnerability.

The growth is in letting yourself be seen enjoying yourself. Trust that joy is a valid emotional destination, not just a rest stop.

---

**Moon in the 6th house.**

Emotional energy goes into the daily — health, routines, and work carry more feeling than usual. The body becomes a messenger: stress shows up physically, and physical care becomes emotional care. Burnout is possible if the body's signals are ignored.

The growth is in learning to feel your way through your systems. What your body needs this year is data worth listening to.

---

**Moon in the 7th house.**

Relationships carry emotional intensity. Partnerships become a mirror — what you feel in close relation with others tells you something about yourself. There's a push and pull to significant relationships this year, and it's purposeful.

The growth is in relating without losing yourself.

---

**Moon in the 8th house.**

This is a deep one. Emotional intensity centers on transformation, loss, power dynamics, and whatever has been pushed below the surface. What's been buried is surfacing. Other people's emotional lives intersect with yours in significant ways.

The Moon in the 8th asks you to feel what you've been avoiding — because what's unexamined in the emotional basement tends to run the show from down there anyway. The growth is in bringing it up into the light.

---

**Moon in the 9th house.**

Emotional growth comes through expansion — through travel, study, or a fundamental shift in your belief system. You're seeking something bigger than your current framework. The restlessness you feel is pointing you toward what you actually believe versus what you've inherited or assumed.

The growth is in following the questions far enough.

---

**Moon in the 10th house.**

Emotion meets ambition. Career, reputation, and your place in the world carry real emotional weight this year. How you feel about your work — whether it aligns with who you're becoming — is a live and sometimes uncomfortable question.

The growth is in holding both: not letting emotion derail the work, and not letting work suppress the emotion. They're both real.

---

**Moon in the 11th house.**

Emotional intensity moves into community and friendship. Your sense of belonging — whether you have it, whether you want it, whether you're finding your people — is a live emotional question. Groups, friendships, and collective work all carry feeling.

The growth is in what it actually means to belong somewhere — and the courage to seek it out honestly.

---

**Moon in the 12th house.**

The emotional work this year is mostly invisible — happening in the unconscious, in dreams, in solitude, in the gaps between one thing and the next. What surfaces may be old, unprocessed, or hard to name.

The Moon in the 12th asks you to feel your way into the dark without needing to immediately understand what you find. The growth is quiet and slow and real. Trust the process even when — especially when — you can't see the shape of it yet.

---

### The Moon's herbal allies

We covered the Sun's plant allies back in Module 02. Now here are the Moon's.

The Moon corresponds to emotional life, cycles, intuition, and the body's rhythms. Her plants carry those same qualities — cooling, nourishing, quietly powerful.

Moon herbs: anise seed, cabbage, chickweed, clary, cleavers, cucumbers, ginger, jasmine.

Work with these in whatever way feels true to you. A jasmine tea before bed. Clary sage in a diffuser during emotional heavy-lifting. Ginger when you need grounding. These are plants for the inner work — for the processing, the feeling, the quiet tending to what the Moon is pointing at this year.

Correspondences, not prescriptions. Use what calls to you. Leave what doesn't.

---

### Reading them together

The SR Sun and SR Moon rarely tell the same story — and that's the point. The Sun is where you're directing your conscious energy. The Moon is where the emotional undercurrent is pulling you. Sometimes they're working in the same direction. Often they're in tension.

That tension is useful information, not a problem to solve.

Here's an example: Sun in the 10th and Moon in the 4th. Your energy is directed toward career and public life — you're being called out into the world. And your emotional growth is happening at home, in the private sphere, in the roots. The push and pull between public and private is the central theme of the year. How do you show up fully out there while tending to what matters most in here?

Or: Sun in the 5th and Moon in the 8th. You're energized by creativity and joy — and the emotional work is transformation and depth. Both can coexist. In fact, deep creative work often requires exactly that: the willingness to go into the dark in order to make something that matters.

Where the Sun and Moon are in conversation — that's where your year is really happening.

Sit with both placements. Note any tension. Notice what they're asking of you together.
