## Why you need to know this

Here's the thing. When you see a line connecting two planets in your chart, you need to know what both of those planets want. When a planet is sitting in your 7th house, you need to know what that planet's energy brings to your relationships. When Saturn is sitting right on top of your Sun — conjunct, merged, unavoidable — you need to know what that means for your year before you can do anything with the information.

The planets are the characters. The signs are their costumes. The houses are the rooms they're standing in.

You can't read the story without knowing the characters. That's what this module is for.

## The Luminaries

### The Sun

The Sun is where you're most alive. It's your core vitality, your essential self, the part of you that has to express itself or you start to feel hollow.

In a natal chart, it's who you are at your center. In a solar return, it's the main event of the year — the house the Sun falls in is where your life force is most concentrated and where the year's central story is being written.

The Sun doesn't ask. It needs. Feed it, and the year has a backbone. Ignore it, and even good things can feel strangely flat.

We covered the SR Sun in depth back in Module 04 — but now you know it's one of the most important characters on the whole stage.

### The Moon

The Moon is your inner world. Your emotional body, your instincts, the parts of yourself that don't think before they feel. It governs what nourishes you, what you need to feel safe, and the rhythms you move through. It's not always rational. It doesn't need to be.

In a solar return, the Moon tells you where your emotional growth is happening — which area of life is asking you to feel your way through it rather than think your way through it. The Moon's house is where you're tender this year. Honor that.

Again — covered in depth in Module 04. I'm including them here so you have the full picture in one place.

## The Personal Planets

These three move quickly and vary a lot from chart to chart. They're the ones that feel most like *you* specifically.

### Mercury

Mercury is how you think and how you talk. Not the big sweeping philosophical mind — that's Jupiter's territory. Mercury is the quick, practical, everyday mind that processes information, forms words, makes connections, writes the text, reads the room. It's your internal wiring.

It also rules everything connected to information and movement: conversations, agreements, short travel, contracts, learning, data. Where Mercury lands in your solar return is where your mind is most active and where communication becomes most significant this year.

If Mercury is conjunct your SR Sun — meaning it's sitting right next to it, merged with it — your mind is deeply fused with your year's central theme. You might think about something obsessively, or do a lot of writing, speaking, or learning that connects directly to the year's main story.

### Venus

Venus is what you love and what you're drawn to. Not just romance — Venus rules the magnetic pull toward beauty, pleasure, comfort, and connection in general. It's also your values: what you consider worth having, worth building, worth keeping.

Venus doesn't force or fight. It magnetizes. Where Venus is placed, things tend to soften. Relationships improve. There's a quality of ease and grace in that area of life. The shadow: Venus can also indicate where you're avoiding discomfort in favor of keeping things pleasant.

The house Venus occupies this year is where love, beauty, and pleasure are most alive for you.

### Mars

Mars is your engine. The part of you that wants, that acts, that goes after things. Raw desire, physical energy, ambition, anger — all Mars. It's not subtle, and it doesn't apologize for existing.

When Mars is well-directed, it's how things actually get done. When it's unfocused, it turns into friction, impatience, or conflict looking for a target.

Mars isn't a problem to solve. It's a force to work with. The house it occupies in your solar return is where that force is most concentrated this year — and most in need of a channel. Give it a direction. Undirected Mars doesn't rest — it just finds its own outlet.

## The Social Planets

Jupiter and Saturn bridge the personal and the collective. They move more slowly — staying in a sign for about a year (Jupiter) or two to three years (Saturn) — so their house placement in a solar return tells you something specific and significant.

### Jupiter

Jupiter is the great expander. Wherever it goes, it makes things bigger — more, larger, abundant. It brings luck in the truest sense: not random fortune, but the kind of open-skies feeling where opportunities multiply and things seem to go your way with less effort than usual. Jupiter is generous. It wants to give.

The other side of Jupiter: it also expands what's already there, including what you don't want. Too much of a good thing is still Jupiter. Overconfidence, excess, spreading too thin — these are Jupiter's shadow.

Jupiter's house in your solar return is one of the most welcome placements you can find. That area of life is opening up this year. Lean into it — but stay grounded. Jupiter's gifts are real. They also require you to know when enough is enough.

### Saturn

Saturn is the most misunderstood planet in the solar system. People hear Saturn and immediately brace themselves. But Saturn is not a punishment. It's a craftsperson.

It rules discipline, structure, limitation, and the kind of slow-earned mastery that can't be faked or rushed. Saturn doesn't hand things to you. It makes you build them properly, with the right foundation, in the right order. What you build under Saturn's eye actually holds.

The house Saturn occupies in your solar return is where the year is asking you to do the real work. It may feel heavier there, slower, more demanding. That's not a sign something is wrong — it's a sign something is being built to last. Saturn's lessons are the ones you carry for decades.

## The Outer Planets

Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto move so slowly through the zodiac that they spend years — sometimes decades — in the same sign. That means their *sign* in your solar return is largely generational, not personal.

What matters with the outer planets is their *house placement* and their *aspects to your personal planets*. That's where their energy becomes specifically yours for the year.

### Uranus

Uranus is the planet of sudden change. It disrupts, breaks down, and breaks open — often in ways you didn't see coming and wouldn't have chosen. But Uranus doesn't break things randomly. It breaks what was keeping you small. It breaks what had become a cage you'd gotten comfortable in. Uranus is the great liberator, even when it doesn't feel like one in the moment.

Uranus's house this year is where the rug might get pulled — and where, if you're paying attention, something more alive is underneath.

Resist the urge to immediately stabilize everything when Uranus is active. Some of what it breaks open is supposed to stay open.

### Neptune

Neptune dissolves. That's its primary function — breaking down the hard edges between things. In its highest expression, it's transcendence: spiritual connection, creative inspiration, mystical experience, compassion without limits. In its more difficult expression, it's confusion, escapism, and idealization that leads to disappointment.

Neptune doesn't lie outright. It blurs. It makes things shimmer and seem more beautiful — or more frightening — than they are.

Neptune's house this year is where clarity may be harder to come by — and where something sacred might be asking to be felt rather than figured out. Dream here. And verify what matters.

### Pluto

Pluto is the planet of irreversible change. Not the quick disruption of Uranus — Pluto is the deep, slow dismantling and rebuilding of entire structures. It rules power, transformation, the unconscious, and everything that lives in the shadows waiting to be brought into the light.

Pluto doesn't negotiate. When it activates an area of your life, that area is being fundamentally changed. What can't hold will go. What can hold will be tested until it proves itself.

The person you are on the other side of a Pluto transit is not the same person you were going in. That's the point.

Pluto's house this year is where the deepest transformation is happening. Trust the process, even when you can't see the shape of what's being built yet.

## How to use this in your chart

You don't need to track every planet equally. Some are doing significant work this year — sitting in important houses, making strong aspects, ruling your SR Ascendant. Others are quieter. Focus your attention on the ones that are most active.

A few things to look for:

**Prominent placements** — a planet in your 1st, 4th, 7th, or 10th house is making its presence felt loudly. Those are the angular houses. Planets there are impossible to ignore.

**Conjunctions with the Sun or Moon** — when any planet is sitting directly on your SR Sun or SR Moon, their energies are merged for the year. That planet's themes are woven into your core focus and emotional experience.

**Your chart ruler** — the planet that rules your SR Rising sign is the second key to your year. You know this from Module 03. Wherever that planet is sitting, it's co-piloting.

**Stelliums** — three or more planets in the same house. If you have a stellium, that house is the year's center of gravity, full stop.
