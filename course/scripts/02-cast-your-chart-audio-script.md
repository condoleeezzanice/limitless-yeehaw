### Before we start — four things you need

First, let's make sure you have what you need.

There are four pieces of information we're working with.

One: your birth date. Day, month, year. Easy.

Two: your birth time — as exact as you can get it. Your birth certificate is the gold standard. A parent's memory, a baby book, hospital records — any of those work. Even a rough hour is useful. If you genuinely have no idea, we'll use noon as a placeholder, and I'll tell you where to take things with a grain of salt when we get there. A missing birth time is not a reason to skip this. Work with what you have.

Three: your birth city. The actual city or town where you were born — not where you grew up, where you were born.

Four: where you'll be on your birthday. This is the one that surprises people. Let me explain why it matters before we log on.

---

### Why your birthday location matters

When the sun returns to its natal position — that moment in time is fixed. It happens when it happens, regardless of where you are.

But a chart is a snapshot of the sky from a specific place on earth. Change the location, change the chart. The planets stay in the same signs, but the houses — the areas of life those planets fall into — those shift depending on where you're standing.

So your solar return chart is cast for where you are on your birthday, not where you were born.

If you spent your birthday at home, use home. If you were traveling, use wherever you were. If your birthday hasn't happened yet, use where you plan to be.

Some astrologers actually travel on their birthday specifically to change the chart — to move a planet into a better house, or out of a difficult one. That's a technique called solar return relocation, and it's real, and we're not going deep on it here. But I wanted you to know it exists so you understand why that birthday location field is not optional.

Okay. Let's pull this chart.

### The walkthrough

I'm going to take you through this step by step. If you want to pause the audio and follow along in real time, do that. There's no wrong way.

**Step one.** Go to astro.com. Just type it in directly.

**Step two.** Look for "Free Horoscopes" in the top navigation. Hover over it or tap it, and from the menu that appears, click "Extended Chart Selection."

**Step three.** You'll land on a form. Fill in your birth information — name is optional, but add your date, time, and place of birth. For the city field, start typing and use the autocomplete dropdown. Make sure you actually select the city from the list rather than just leaving what you typed. That detail matters for the coordinates.

**Step four.** Scroll down past your birth data to the chart options section. You're looking for a dropdown labeled something like "Chart type" or "Method." Click it. Scroll through until you find "Solar Return." Select that.

**Step five.** A year field should now appear. Choose the year you're casting for. If your birthday hasn't happened yet this calendar year, use this year. If your birthday has already passed, use next year — that's your upcoming solar return.

**Step six.** Find the location field for the solar return. This is distinct from your birth city. Enter the city where you'll be on your birthday. Use the autocomplete again. Double-check that it's not still showing your birth city — it's easy to overlook.

**Step seven.** Hit the generate button. It's usually orange, usually says something like "Click here to show the chart." Go ahead and click.

Your chart will load. It's a circle divided into wedges, symbols scattered throughout, lines crossing in the middle. That's exactly right. That's your year.

### What you're looking at

Let's do a quick orientation.

The big circle is the chart wheel. It's divided into twelve sections — those are the houses, the areas of life. The symbols inside those sections are the planets and points. The lines through the center are the aspects — the relationships between planets.

Along the outer edge of the wheel, you'll see glyphs marking where signs change. Inside the wedges, the symbols show which planets have landed in each section.

I know it looks like a lot. It is a lot. That's why we're doing this piece by piece.

You only need to find three things right now.

---

### Your three landmarks

We're going to spend the next several modules going deep on these three, but here's where to find them today.

**First: your SR Rising sign.** On the left side of the chart wheel, look for a horizontal line pointing left. You'll see the letters "AC" or "ASC" near it — that's the Ascendant. The sign sitting right there is your SR Rising. It sets the tone for the whole year.

**Second: your SR Sun.** The Sun glyph is a circle with a dot in the center — looks like a target, basically. Find it on the wheel. Note which house it's in — which section — and which sign. The Sun is the most important placement in the solar return chart. It's telling you where your core energy and focus are going this year.

**Third: your SR Moon.** The Moon is a crescent. Find it, note its house and sign. The SR Moon points to your area of emotional focus and growth — where the work is, and where the evolution is.

Look for AC or ASC for your Rising. The circle with a dot for your Sun. The crescent for your Moon.

Write those down if you want — house number and sign for each. That's your foundation. That's what we're building from.
