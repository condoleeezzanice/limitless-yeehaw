## The planetary week

Okay. Here's a thing you already know but probably never thought about: the days of the week are named for the planets.

Sunday — Sun. Monday — Moon. Tuesday — Mars. Wednesday — Mercury. Thursday — Jupiter. Friday — Venus. Saturday — Saturn.

This is not a coincidence. It's a remnant of a time when planetary timing was woven into daily life. The day you start something carries the energy of that planet.

So.

Thursday is Jupiter's day. Jupiter is about expansion, abundance, growth. If you're starting something you want to grow — a project, a business, a creative endeavor — Thursday gives it that expansive energy to work with.

Friday is Venus. Good for creative work, connection, beauty, anything that involves relating to people or making something lovely.

Tuesday is Mars. Good for action, for confronting something that needs to be confronted, for getting things done and not second-guessing yourself.

Saturday is Saturn. Good for structure, discipline, and laying the kind of groundwork that holds.

This isn't superstition. It's timing. And timing is one of the most underused tools in any practice.

## The planetary hour

It goes even deeper.

Each day is divided into planetary hours. Not clock hours — planetary hours, which shift with sunrise and sunset. Every hour within the day has its own ruler, cycling through all seven classical planets in a specific order. The first hour of each day always belongs to that day's ruling planet. And then the cycle continues from there.

Which means you can get incredibly specific. You don't just choose the right day — you choose the right *hour* within the day for what you're trying to do.

Here's an example. Let's say you want to start something that involves expanding your reach — a pitch, a creative project, getting your ideas out into the world in a bigger way.

You'd start on a Thursday — Jupiter's day — during the *hour of Mercury*. Jupiter gives it room to grow. Mercury gives it a voice. Two layers of timing, working together. That's planetary magic. That's astrological alchemy.

Now — calculating planetary hours by hand is tedious, and you don't need to do it. There's an app called **Planetaro** that does this for you. It tells you the planetary ruler of the current day and each hour within it, in real time. It's the tool I use.

Before you start something that matters, open it. See what hour you're in. See what day it is. You don't have to restructure your entire life around it. But when you have the *choice* of when to begin something? Let the planets help you choose.

## The planets and the body

People have been mapping the planets onto the human body for as long as they've been reading the stars. Every planet corresponds to specific physical systems — organs, glands, structures that carry that same quality of energy.

This is one of the foundations of medical astrology and herbal healing traditions. And I want to say very clearly: this is *not* a replacement for working with doctors and medical practitioners. Please do that. This is a layer of awareness — not a diagnosis.

But here's where it gets interesting for your solar return specifically.

When a planet is strongly activated in your chart — sitting in an angular house, making powerful aspects, conjunct your Sun or Moon — the body system it rules may need extra attention that year. Not fear. Care.

Let me give you the list.

The **Sun** rules the anterior pituitary gland, the heart and arteries, blood circulation.

The **Moon** rules the alimentary canal, female reproductive organs, lymph glands, the cerebellum, and the sympathetic nervous system.

**Mercury** rules the brain and cerebrospinal nervous system.

**Venus** rules the thymus gland and the kidneys.

**Mars** rules the cortical portion of the adrenal glands and the muscles.

**Jupiter** rules the posterior pituitary gland, the liver and blood plasma.

**Saturn** rules parts of the adrenal glands, the bones, teeth, joints, tendons, and spleen.

**Uranus** rules the parathyroid gland and the brain and cerebrospinal system — which it shares with Mercury.

**Neptune** rules the pineal gland.

**Pluto** rules the pancreas, digestive glands, and sexual organs.

So — look at your solar return. Which planets are most active this year? Those body systems are worth your attention. Nourish what the year is working hard.
