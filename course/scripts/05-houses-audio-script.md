### The houses, revisited

In Module 01, we introduced the four pieces of the chart: signs, planets, houses, and aspects. In Modules 03 and 04, you used the houses to read your SR Rising, SR Sun, and SR Moon.

Now we go further. Because the houses aren't just a backdrop — they're active. Every planet in your solar return chart has landed somewhere, and where it landed tells you where that planet's energy is showing up in your actual life this year.

Think of the twelve houses as twelve rooms in a house. The planets are the people in those rooms — each one bringing their particular energy into that particular space. The Sun in the 10th is doing something completely different than the Sun in the 4th. Mars in the 2nd is doing something different than Mars in the 7th. Same planet, different arena.

The house tells you where. The planet tells you what. The sign tells you how. All three together give you the full sentence.

### How to read any planet in any house

The principle is simple, even if the combinations are endless.

Each planet represents a facet of life and experience. Each house represents an area of life. When a planet lands in a house, that facet of life is activated in that arena.

Let me give you a few examples.

Venus in the 6th. Venus governs love, pleasure, beauty, and connection. The 6th house is daily routines, health, and work. Put them together: love of the work itself, finding pleasure in the everyday, beauty as part of the health routine. Relationships with coworkers may be warm this year.

Mars in the 3rd. Mars governs drive, action, and conflict. The 3rd house is communication, learning, and the local world. Put them together: assertive communication, passionate ideas, debates that sharpen your thinking. The mind is on fire this year.

Jupiter in the 2nd. Jupiter governs expansion, luck, and abundance. The 2nd house is money and self-worth. Put them together: financial growth, an expanding sense of what you're worth, a year where generosity comes back around.

Saturn in the 5th. Saturn governs discipline and long-term building. The 5th house is creativity, romance, and pleasure. Put them together: creative work that requires serious effort and commitment. Not effortless — but deeply worthwhile.

That's how it works. What does this planet bring, and where is it bringing it? Layer in the sign for the how, and you have the whole sentence.

### What about empty houses?

Most houses in your solar return chart will have no planets in them. That's completely normal — there are ten planets and twelve houses, so at minimum two will always be empty. Often six or seven are.

An empty house isn't absent — it just won't be a main focus of effort. It quietly carries the texture of its sign without demanding anything from you.

Planets show you where the year asks for your attention. Empty houses aren't voids — they're still present, still flavored by their sign, just not asking for your direct focus.

Start with the houses that are occupied — those are where the year's main energy lives. The empty ones are still part of the picture.

### The 12 houses in depth

Okay. Here are all twelve houses — what each one governs, and what it means when planets land there. When you hear your house, really listen.

---

**The 1st house — the house of self.**

The most personal point in the chart. Your body, your personality, your instinctive way of moving through the world. In the solar return, the 1st house is where you're reinventing yourself or bringing your attention back to who you actually are.

Any planet here is front-facing and energized. Its themes are personal and immediate — you embody them, whether you intend to or not.

---

**The 2nd house — the house of resources.**

Everything you have, and everything you think you're worth. Money, possessions, and material security. But also self-worth — which tends to be the more interesting story.

Any planet here activates your financial life and your sense of what you deserve. Expect that planet's themes to show up in how you earn, spend, or feel about what you have.

---

**The 3rd house — the house of mind and movement.**

How you think, how you communicate, how you make sense of your immediate world. Writing, speaking, learning, short trips, siblings, the daily flow of information through your life.

Any planet here influences your mental life and communication style. Your words carry that planet's energy this year.

---

**The 4th house — the house of roots.**

The bottom of the chart and the bottom of the self. Home, family, ancestry, and the emotional foundation you stand on. What you go home to, and what lives in you that you didn't choose.

Planets in the 4th are working in the private sphere — in the family system, in the internal emotional architecture of your life. The work here tends not to be visible to the world. It's real nonetheless.

---

**The 5th house — the house of creation and joy.**

Where you come alive. Creativity, play, romance, children, performance, and the pleasure of being in a body that can make things and fall in love. The things you do for their own sake.

Any planet here energizes your creative life, romantic life, or capacity for pleasure. The themes of that planet show up in what you make and who you're drawn to.

---

**The 6th house — the house of daily life.**

The machinery of the everyday. Health, routines, the quality of your work, and how you show up in service to something. Unglamorous and essential. Where discipline becomes craft and where the body keeps score.

Any planet here influences your health, routines, and work habits. That planet's themes will make themselves known in how your body feels and how your days run.

---

**The 7th house — the house of partnership.**

The house of the other. Who you're in significant relationship with, what you're building together. Romantic partners, business partners, close collaborators, contracts. The 7th also holds the concept of open opposition — people who actively challenge you.

Any planet here activates your significant relationships. You'll meet that planet's themes in the people who come into your life or intensify in it.

---

**The 8th house — the house of transformation.**

Where things get real. Transformation, shared resources, debt, power, sexuality, the unconscious, and everything that involves merging your life with someone else's. Death and rebirth — not always literally. The things you can't fully control and can't look away from.

Any planet here invites that planet's themes into your deepest, most transformative experiences. The work is real and the change is lasting.

---

**The 9th house — the house of expansion.**

Where you reach beyond what you already know. Higher education, philosophy, long-distance travel, publishing, belief systems, the search for meaning. The house of the horizon.

Any planet here expands its themes outward and upward. Travel, study, publishing, or a significant shift in your worldview may bring that planet's energy forward. The themes go big this year.

---

**The 10th house — the house of public life.**

The most public point in the chart. Career, reputation, achievement, authority, and what you're building that the world can see. What you're known for and what you want to be known for.

Any planet here brings that planet's energy into your public and professional sphere. What that planet is doing this year — the world may notice.

---

**The 11th house — the house of community.**

Where the individual meets the collective. Friends, social networks, groups, collective causes, the larger vision of what could be, your hopes and long-term wishes.

Any planet here activates your social world and collective connections. The themes of that planet will show up in who you're building with and whether you feel like you belong somewhere real.

---

**The 12th house — the house of the unseen.**

Everything that happens below or behind. The unconscious, the hidden, the spiritual. Solitude, retreat, dreams, what's not yet ready to be named. Endings that make space for what comes next.

Planets in the 12th are working quietly — often invisibly. Their themes are moving in the background of the year. What that planet is doing may not be visible from the outside, but it's shaping something real.

### The six house axes

Now — the houses aren't twelve isolated rooms. They're six pairs of opposites. Each house sits across the wheel from another, and they're in conversation.

When one house is active, the house across from it is also involved — whether or not anything is explicitly landing there. The tension is built in, and it's informative.

Here are the six axes:

**1st and 7th: self versus other.** Who I am, versus who I am in relation to you. What you bring to relationship and what you find in it.

**2nd and 8th: mine versus ours.** What I own, versus what we share. Individual security versus the intimacy of entanglement.

**3rd and 9th: local versus distant.** The immediate world versus the horizon. Everyday learning versus philosophy and big-picture meaning.

**4th and 10th: private versus public.** Home versus career. The roots versus the branches. The pull between these two is one of the most common tensions in a solar return.

**5th and 11th: personal joy versus collective hope.** What I create for myself, versus what I create for us. Self-expression versus contribution to something larger.

**6th and 12th: visible versus invisible work.** The daily, practical, measurable work, versus the inner, unconscious, unmeasurable work. Both are necessary. Neither is more real.

When you have planets clustered on one side of an axis, look to the opposite house for the counterweight — the area quietly demanding attention even if nothing is explicitly landing there.

Reading a chart is a Mercury activity — making connections, interpreting symbols, moving between pieces of information to find the pattern underneath.

Mercury is the planet of the mind: how we think, how we communicate, how we learn. As you work through your solar return chart, these are the plants that carry Mercury's energy. Good company for the intellectual work.

### Starting to see the whole picture

By now you've identified your SR Rising, SR Sun, SR Moon, your ruling planet, and you've read each placement in its house. The year is taking shape.

This is a good moment to step back and look at the chart as a whole.

Where are the planets clustered? Mostly in the upper half of the chart — houses seven through twelve — that suggests a year that's more public, outward-facing, engaged with the world. Mostly in the lower half — houses one through six — that's a year that's more personal, private, inward.

Mostly on the left side of the chart — personal initiative is driving the year. Mostly on the right — circumstances and other people have more influence.

These are loose patterns, not rigid rules. But they give you a sense of the year's overall orientation. Which direction life is pulling. Where most of the action is concentrated.

The chart is a whole sentence, not a collection of separate words. The planets, signs, and houses all speak to each other. You're learning to hear the conversation.
