### First: your natal chart

You've probably heard of a birth chart. That's your natal chart — a map of exactly where every planet was in the sky the moment you were born.

It's fixed. It doesn't change. It's the foundational blueprint of who you are: your instincts, your gifts, your patterns, the way you move through the world.

Think of it as the original document.

We're not going deep on natal charts in this course — that's a whole universe of its own, and it deserves its own dedicated time. But you need to know it exists, because what we're actually working with is something different.

### What is a solar return?

Your solar return is when the sun goes back to the same position it was in when you were born.

That happens every year, right around your birthday. And when it does, something resets.

It's a return to the blueprint of your warmth.

Your solar return chart is a snapshot of where all the planets are at that exact moment — the sun's homecoming. And from that snapshot, you get a map. A map of the year ahead, and the energies available to you.

It's not a prediction. It's not fate. It's more like a weather report for your soul — here's what's in the atmosphere. Here's what you're working with.

One important detail: you cast your solar return chart for wherever you are on your birthday — not where you were born. That matters. We'll get into exactly how in the next module when we actually cast it together.

### The four pieces of the chart

Okay. When you look at a solar return chart for the first time, it can feel like a lot. There's a circle, there are symbols everywhere, there are lines crossing through the middle.

Here's all you need to know right now. There are four things to understand. Just four.

**Signs.** The 12 signs of the zodiac — Aries through Pisces — are flavors of energy. They describe how something operates. Aries is bold and initiating. Cancer is nurturing and protective. Scorpio is deep and transformative. We'll get to know the ones that matter most for your chart as we go.

**Planets.** Each planet represents a different part of life and experience. The Sun is your core focus and energy for the year. The Moon is your emotional world. Mercury is how you think and communicate. We'll walk through each one as it becomes relevant.

**Houses.** The chart is divided into 12 sections called houses. Each house corresponds to a different area of your life — relationships, career, home, health, and so on. When a planet lands in a house, it tells you where that energy is showing up for you this year.

**Aspects.** Aspects are the lines drawn between planets on the chart. They tell you how the planets are relating to each other. Some create ease and flow — the energies work together naturally. Some create friction — a challenge, something to work through or master. Neither is bad. Both are useful.

Signs, planets, houses, aspects. That's the whole grammar of the chart. You don't need to memorize anything right now — just know these four words, and you'll have a hook to hang everything else on.
