### What your SR Rising sign tells you

In Module 02, you found the letters "AC" or "ASC" on the left side of your chart wheel. The sign sitting at that point is your SR Rising — your solar return Ascendant.

In astrology, the Ascendant is always about how something presents itself. In your natal chart, it's how you present yourself — your instinctive style, your first impression. In your solar return chart, the Ascendant does the same thing — but for the year instead of for your life.

It sets the tone. It describes the role you're stepping into, the energy or attitude you'll be embodying, the general theme or mood of the next twelve months. It's what others will start to notice about you. It's how you'll tend to initiate new experiences.

Think of it as the costume the year is wearing. It shapes everything you encounter while you're in it.

Now — it's not the most important placement in the solar return chart. That's the SR Sun, which we cover in Module 04. But it's the frame. And knowing the frame changes how you read everything inside it.

---

### A note if you're working without a birth time

Quick note before we get to the signs: the Rising is the placement most affected by an uncertain birth time. The Ascendant moves through all twelve signs in a single day, so even an hour's difference can shift it.

If you're working from an estimated birth time, read the sign that appeared on your chart — but hold it lightly. Notice if it resonates. If it doesn't feel right at all, look at the signs on either side of it too.

Everything else in this module — the ruling planet, where it lands — still applies and is still worth your time.

### The 12 SR Rising signs

Okay. Find your SR Rising sign. I'm going to go through all twelve. When we get to yours, really listen — read it as the territory you're moving through this year, not as a personality type, and not as a prediction.

The herbs I mention for each sign are that sign's plant allies for the year. Work with them however resonates.

---

**Aries Rising.**

You're stepping into a year of bold initiation. Aries rising sets an energized, action-forward tone — you're showing up first, moving fast, willing to start things without a roadmap. There's a sharpness to you this year, a readiness to lead or go first when no one else will.

The theme is beginnings. New territory. Courage as something you practice, not just feel. This is a year to move toward what you want without waiting for conditions to be perfect. They won't be. Move anyway.

Herbs for Aries: betony, blackberry, blessed thistle, chili powder, cowslips, juniper, loosestrife, marjoram, mushrooms.

---

**Taurus Rising.**

The year slows down into something deliberate. Taurus rising brings a grounded, sensory, accumulating quality to everything — you're building, settling in, paying attention to what you actually have. Others notice a steadiness to you, a new attentiveness to comfort and beauty and what's worth keeping.

The theme is patience as a practice, pleasure as a valid tool. What are you cultivating this year? What do you actually value? This is a year to move at your own pace and mean it.

Herbs for Taurus: apple blossom, cumin, fig-wort, lovage.

---

**Gemini Rising.**

Quick, curious, communicative. Gemini rising sets a year of information-gathering, connection-making, and moving fluidly between worlds. You're showing up as someone who asks good questions, notices patterns, adapts fast. Others notice your words, your wit, your ability to be genuinely interested in almost anything.

The theme is learning and expressing — thinking out loud as part of the work. This is a year where the conversation itself is the destination.

Herbs for Gemini: dragon's blood reeds, meadowsweet.

---

**Cancer Rising.**

The year turns inward and tender. Cancer rising brings a protective, emotionally attuned quality to how you're showing up — you're leading with feeling, with care, with an instinct to safeguard what matters. Home, belonging, roots, and the people who feel like family are all front and center.

Others may notice a softer edge to you, or a stronger impulse to protect your inner world. The theme is nourishment — of yourself, of the relationships and places you love, of whatever you're quietly tending to.

Herbs for Cancer: adder's tongue, black alder, agrimony, balm, water betony, daisies, duckweed, honeysuckle, hyssop, liverwort.

---

**Leo Rising.**

You're being called forward — into visibility, into warmth, into full self-expression. Leo rising sets a year where showing up fully is the assignment. Others notice your presence, your generosity, your creative spark. There's a brightness to you this year that's hard to miss.

The theme is radiance — not performance, not seeking approval, but authentic shine. This is a year to take up space without apology. The world benefits when you stop dimming.

Herbs for Leo: agaric, angelica, bay tree, borage, goat's rue, oats, peony, rue, rye, saffron, sage, st. john's wort, wheat.

---

**Virgo Rising.**

The year asks for discernment, craft, and care. Virgo rising brings a focused, analytical, service-oriented quality to how you move through the world. You're attending to the details — what's working, what needs refinement, what you're called to improve. Others notice your precision, your reliability, your high standards.

The theme is skill — doing the thing well, not just doing it. This is a year where the quality of your attention becomes the quality of your output.

Herbs for Virgo: lavender, maidenhair.

---

**Libra Rising.**

Partnership, beauty, and balance move to the foreground. Libra rising sets a year where relationship — with others, with aesthetics, with fairness — is the operating system. You're showing up as a collaborator, a connector, someone who cares how things look and feel and whether they're working for everyone involved.

Others notice your grace, your consideration, your eye for what's beautiful. The theme is relating — what you're creating alongside other people, and what it costs you to keep the peace versus what it costs you to stop.

Herbs for Libra: rose geranium, horse-tongue, verbena, vervain.

**Scorpio Rising.**

Depth, intensity, and transformation. Scorpio rising sets a year where the surface isn't the point — you're going underneath things, asking harder questions, refusing easy answers and comfortable half-truths. Others notice a magnetic quality to you, a seriousness, a sense of power held close.

The theme is excavation — what's hidden, what's real, what can't be avoided any longer. This is a year of meaningful change. You'll emerge knowing something you can't unknow.

Herbs for Scorpio: basil, bloodroot, dwarf elder.

**Sagittarius Rising.**

The year opens wide. Sagittarius rising brings an expansive, philosophical, freedom-seeking energy to how you're showing up. You're oriented toward the horizon — toward learning, travel, and the bigger picture. Others notice your enthusiasm, your vision, your need for room to roam.

The theme is belief — what you're reaching toward and why, what it means to live according to something larger than your own comfort. This is a year to go further than you planned.

Herbs for Sagittarius: alexander, dandelion.

**Capricorn Rising.**

A year of building with intention. Capricorn rising sets a serious, ambitious, long-game tone. You're showing up as someone who's working toward something — disciplined, strategic, committed to the result. Others notice your focus, your reliability, a quiet authority that wasn't quite there before.

The theme is legacy — what you're constructing that will last, what you're willing to do the unglamorous work for. This is a year where patience and structure pay off.

Herbs for Capricorn: comfrey, moss.

**Aquarius Rising.**

The year breaks from convention. Aquarius rising brings an independent, forward-thinking, collective-minded quality to how you move through the world. You're showing up as someone with their own angle — original, community-oriented, maybe a little detached from the personal in favor of the broader picture. Others notice your distinctness, your ideas, your refusal to follow scripts.

The theme is innovation — what's possible when you stop doing it the expected way, what you can contribute when you're willing to be a little strange.

Herbs for Aquarius: star anise, curry, spikenard, wisteria.

---

**Pisces Rising.**

The year becomes porous and intuitive. Pisces rising sets a dreamy, spiritually attuned, deeply feeling quality to everything. You're showing up with less armor and more permeability — sensing things before you can name them, moving by feel. Others notice a softness, a creativity, something a little otherworldly.

The theme is surrender — trusting what you can't see, following the current instead of fighting it. This is a year for the invisible work. It counts even when it can't be measured.

Herbs for Pisces: common alder, hemp, moss, sphagnum.

### Your ruling planet

Every sign has a ruling planet — a planet that carries that sign's energy most naturally. When a sign becomes your SR Rising, its ruling planet becomes the chart ruler for the year: a second key for reading the theme.

Here are the rulerships — we're using modern astrology's assignments:

Aries — Mars. Taurus — Venus. Gemini — Mercury. Cancer — the Moon. Leo — the Sun. Virgo — Mercury. Libra — Venus. Scorpio — Pluto. Sagittarius — Jupiter. Capricorn — Saturn. Aquarius — Uranus. Pisces — Neptune.

Got yours? Now go find that planet on your chart.

---

### Finding your ruling planet in the chart

Each planet has a glyph. Some of the common ones: the Sun is a circle with a dot — ☉. The Moon is a crescent. Mercury looks like a circle with horns. Venus is the circle with a cross below it. Mars is the circle with an arrow. Jupiter looks like a numeral four. Saturn has a cross with a curved base. Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto are often tiny and clustered — look carefully at the outer edge of the chart.

Once you've found it, note two things: which house it's in, and which sign it's in.

The house your ruling planet occupies is where the year's central theme is playing out. The sign it's in tells you how.

Here's a quick example. Say you have Gemini rising — that makes Mercury your chart ruler. And Mercury is in the 10th house in Capricorn. The year's theme is communication and learning. It's playing out in career and public reputation. And the flavor is serious, disciplined, results-oriented. That's a year where what you say at work, how you present yourself professionally, could really shift things.

You don't need to know every nuance right now. Just find your ruling planet, note where it is, and let that sit alongside what you already know about your rising sign.
