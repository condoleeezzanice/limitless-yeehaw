Module 06 · The Planets

You've seen them on the chart.
Now let's talk about
who they actually are.

Ten planets. Ten kinds of energy. Each one asking something different from your year. This is your field guide.

Why you need to know this

You've spent the last several modules reading your chart by placement — your Rising sign, your Sun, your Moon, which houses are activated. But the chart doesn't just tell you where things are landing. It tells you who is landing there.

When you see a line connecting two planets in your chart, you need to know what both of those planets want. When a planet is sitting in your 7th house, you need to know what that planet's energy means for your relationships. When you notice that Saturn is sitting right on top of your Sun — conjunct, merged, unavoidable — you need to know what that actually means for your year.

The planets are the characters. The signs are their costumes. The houses are the rooms they're standing in. You can't read the story without knowing the characters.

This module is your field guide. We'll go through all ten planets — what each one represents, what it's asking for, and what it means when it shows up prominently in your solar return. By the end, you'll be able to look at any planet on your chart and know exactly what it brings to the year.

The planets, one by one

The Luminaries — already covered in Module 04

☉

The Sun

Vitality · Identity · Core Focus · Rulership: Leo

The Sun is where you're most alive. It's your core vitality, your essential self, the part of you that has to express itself or you start to feel hollow. In a natal chart, it's who you are at your center. In a solar return, it's the main event of the year — the house the Sun falls in is where your life force is most concentrated and where the year's central story is being written.

The Sun doesn't ask. It needs. Feed it, and the year has a backbone. Ignore it, and even good things can feel strangely flat.

In your solar return

The SR Sun's house is your year's main theme. That area of life is where your focus, energy, and identity are most alive. We covered this in depth in Module 04.

☽

The Moon

Emotion · Instinct · Nourishment · Rulership: Cancer

The Moon is your inner world — your emotional body, your instincts, the parts of yourself that don't think before they feel. It governs what nourishes you, what you need to feel safe, and the rhythms you move through. It's not always rational. It doesn't need to be.

In a solar return, the Moon tells you where your emotional growth is happening — which area of life is asking you to feel your way through it rather than think your way through it. The Moon's house is where you're tender this year. Honor it.

In your solar return

The SR Moon's house is where your emotional focus lives this year — where you'll feel things most deeply. We covered this in depth in Module 04.

The Personal Planets — move quickly, highly individual

☿

Mercury

Mind · Communication · Information · Rulership: Gemini & Virgo

Mercury is how you think and how you talk. It rules the mind — not the big sweeping philosophical mind of Jupiter, but the quick, practical, everyday mind that processes information, forms words, makes connections, writes the text, reads the room. Mercury is your internal wiring.

It also rules everything connected to information and movement: conversations, agreements, short travel, contracts, learning, data. Where Mercury lands is where your mind is most active and where communication becomes most significant.

In your solar return

Mercury's house shows where your mental energy is concentrated this year — where you'll be thinking hardest, learning most, or doing your most significant communicating. If Mercury is conjunct your SR Sun, your mind is deeply fused with your year's central theme.

♀

Venus

Love · Beauty · Pleasure · Values · Rulership: Taurus & Libra

Venus is what you love and what you're drawn to. It rules attraction — not just romantic attraction, but the magnetic pull toward beauty, pleasure, comfort, and connection. It's also your values: what you consider worth having, worth building, worth keeping.

Venus doesn't force or fight. It magnetizes. Where Venus is placed, things tend to soften. Relationships improve. Beauty shows up. There's a quality of ease and grace in that area of life. The shadow: Venus can also indicate where you're avoiding discomfort in favor of keeping things pleasant.

In your solar return

Venus's house is where love, beauty, and pleasure are most alive for you this year. It's often where relationships flourish, where creative work feels most satisfying, or where you're learning what you actually value.

♂

Mars

Drive · Desire · Action · Conflict · Rulership: Aries

Mars is your engine. It's the part of you that wants, that acts, that goes after things. Raw desire, physical energy, ambition, anger — all Mars. It's not subtle, and it doesn't apologize for existing. When Mars is well-directed, it's how things actually get done. When it's unfocused, it turns into friction, impatience, or conflict looking for a target.

Mars isn't a problem to solve. It's a force to work with. The house it occupies in your solar return is where that force is most concentrated — and most in need of a channel.

In your solar return

Mars's house is where you're most driven, most assertive, and potentially most prone to conflict this year. It's also where you have the most energy to actually make things happen. Give it a direction. Undirected Mars doesn't rest — it just finds its own outlet.

The Social Planets — bridge between the personal and the collective

♃

Jupiter

Expansion · Abundance · Growth · Luck · Rulership: Sagittarius

Jupiter is the great expander. Wherever it goes, it makes things bigger — more, larger, abundant. It brings luck in the truest sense: not random fortune, but the kind of open-skies feeling where opportunities multiply and things seem to go your way with less effort than usual. Jupiter is generous. It wants to give.

The other side of Jupiter: it also expands what's already there, including what you don't want. Too much of a good thing is still Jupiter. Overconfidence, excess, spreading too thin — these are Jupiter's shadow. The house it occupies is not just a blessing, it's an area of life that's asking you to grow — which means it may also feel like it's asking a lot.

In your solar return

Jupiter's house is one of the most welcome placements in a solar return. That area of life is opening up this year — more opportunity, more abundance, more ease. Lean into it, but stay grounded. Jupiter's gifts are real. They also require you to know when enough is enough.

♄

Saturn

Discipline · Structure · Mastery · Time · Rulership: Capricorn

Saturn is the taskmaster of the solar system — and the most misunderstood. It rules discipline, structure, limitation, and the kind of slow-earned mastery that can't be faked or rushed. Saturn doesn't hand things to you. It makes you build them properly, with the right foundation, in the right order. What you build under Saturn's eye actually holds.

The house Saturn occupies in your solar return is where the year is asking you to do the real work. It may feel heavier there, slower, more demanding. That's not a sign something is wrong — it's a sign that something is being built to last. Saturn's lessons are the ones you carry for decades.

In your solar return

Where Saturn sits this year is where you're being asked to grow up in some respect — to take something seriously, to be more disciplined, to stop avoiding the structural work. It's not punishment. It's an invitation to build something real.

The Outer Planets — generational, slow-moving, transformative

Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto move so slowly through the zodiac that they spend years — sometimes decades — in the same sign. Their sign placement in a solar return is largely generational rather than personal. What matters is their house placement and their aspects to your personal planets. That's where their energy becomes specifically yours for the year.

♅

Uranus

Disruption · Liberation · Innovation · Awakening · Rulership: Aquarius

Uranus is the planet of sudden change. It disrupts, breaks down, and breaks open — often in ways you didn't see coming and wouldn't have chosen. But Uranus doesn't break things randomly. It breaks what was keeping you small. It breaks what had become a cage you'd gotten comfortable in. Uranus is the great liberator, even when it doesn't feel like one in the moment.

It rules innovation, rebellion, and anything that departs from the expected path. Where Uranus lands, expect the unexpected. The house it occupies in your solar return is where the rug might get pulled — and where, if you're paying attention, something more alive is underneath.

In your solar return

Uranus's house is where this year brings sudden shifts, surprises, or a strong pull toward something radically different from the status quo. Resist the urge to immediately stabilize everything. Some of what Uranus breaks open is supposed to stay open.

♆

Neptune

Dreams · Spirituality · Dissolution · Illusion · Rulership: Pisces

Neptune dissolves. That's its primary function — breaking down the hard edges between things, between people, between you and something larger than yourself. In its highest expression, Neptune is transcendence: spiritual connection, creative inspiration, mystical experience, compassion without boundaries. In its more difficult expression, Neptune is confusion, escapism, and idealization that leads to disappointment.

Neptune doesn't lie outright. It blurs. It makes things shimmer and seem more beautiful or more frightening than they are. The house it occupies in your solar return is where clarity may be harder to come by this year — and where something sacred might be asking to be felt rather than figured out.

In your solar return

Neptune's house is where this year is asking for softness, intuition, and a willingness to not have it all pinned down. It's also where you're most susceptible to seeing what you want to see. Dream here — and verify what matters.

♇

Pluto

Transformation · Power · Death & Rebirth · Depth · Rulership: Scorpio

Pluto is the planet of irreversible change. Not the quick disruption of Uranus — Pluto is the deep, slow dismantling and rebuilding of entire structures. It rules power, transformation, the unconscious, and everything that lives in the shadows waiting to be brought into the light. Death and rebirth — literal or metaphorical — are Pluto's domain.

Pluto doesn't negotiate. When it activates an area of your life, that area is being fundamentally changed. What can't hold will go. What can hold will be tested until it proves itself. The person you are on the other side of a Pluto transit is not the same person you were going in. That's the point.

In your solar return

Pluto's house is where the deepest transformation of the year is happening. This isn't surface-level change — it's excavation. Something is being fundamentally remade in that area of your life. Trust the process, even when you can't see the shape of what's being built yet.

How to use this in your chart

You don't need to track every planet in your solar return equally. Some will be doing significant work this year — sitting in important houses, forming strong aspects, ruling your SR Ascendant. Others will be quieter. Focus your attention on the planets that are most active.

Look for

Prominent placements

A planet in your 1st, 4th, 7th, or 10th house (the angular houses) is making its presence felt loudly this year. Planets in the 1st are especially significant — they color the whole year's experience.

Look for

Conjunctions with the Sun or Moon

When any planet is sitting directly on your SR Sun or SR Moon, their energies are merged for the year. That planet's themes are woven into your core focus and your emotional experience. We go deep on this in the next module.

Look for

Your chart ruler

The planet that rules your SR Rising sign is the second key to your year. Wherever that planet is sitting — whatever house, whatever aspects — it's co-piloting the year alongside your SR Rising. You already know this from Module 03.

Look for

Stelliums

Three or more planets in the same house form a stellium — a concentration of energy in one area of life so dense it becomes impossible to ignore. If you have a stellium, that house is the year's center of gravity, full stop.

The chart is showing you where the energy is. The planets tell you what kind of energy it is. Together, they give you the full picture.

In the next module, we'll look at aspects — the lines between planets — and you'll see how knowing each planet's energy lets you read those relationships with real depth. A trine between Jupiter and Venus means something very different than a trine between Saturn and Mars. The aspect type tells you the quality. The planets tell you the story.
