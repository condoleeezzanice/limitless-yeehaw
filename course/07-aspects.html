Module 07 · Aspects: Lines on the Chart

The planets aren't alone.
They're in conversation.
Here's how to hear it.

Aspects are the relationships between planets — where the energies support each other, challenge each other, or merge into something new. This is the last piece.

What aspects are

Look at the center of your chart wheel. Those lines crossing through the middle — each one connects two planets. Those connections are aspects.

An aspect is formed when two planets sit at a meaningful angular distance from each other — 60 degrees apart, 90 degrees, 120 degrees, and so on. Different angles create different kinds of relationships between the planets involved. Some create ease. Some create friction. Some merge the energies entirely.

You've already been reading planets individually — what each one is doing in its house, in its sign. Aspects add the relational layer: how are these planets talking to each other? Are they supporting each other? Pulling in opposite directions? Amplifying each other's energy to the point of overwhelm?

Aspects are where the chart stops being a list of ingredients and starts being a recipe. The planets interact. What happens between them shapes the whole year.

On astro.com, the lines in the center of the wheel are color-coded. Blue or green lines typically indicate ease and flow. Red lines typically indicate friction and challenge. The legend may vary slightly depending on your settings, but this is the general convention. Use the colors as a first read, then we'll go deeper on what each type means.

The five major aspects

There are many aspects in astrology, but five are the most significant and the ones you'll encounter most often. These are what we're working with.

☌ 0°

Conjunction

Merging

Two planets in the same place in the sky — sitting right on top of each other. When planets conjoin, their energies don't just interact. They blend. They merge into something unified. The two become one force operating together in the same house, the same sign, toward the same area of life.

Whether this is easy or difficult depends on which planets are involved. Two compatible planets conjunct each other can be powerfully concentrated energy. Two incompatible planets can feel like being pulled apart while standing in the same spot. But even the difficult conjunctions have something to teach — because you can't separate the energies and deal with them one at a time. You have to work with both at once.

⚹ 60°

Sextile

Ease · Opportunity

Two planets 60 degrees apart. Sextiles are harmonious — the energies involved have access to each other and tend to work well together. They create a kind of mutual support: what one planet is doing makes it easier for the other to do its thing.

Sextiles are gentler than trines — they offer opportunity rather than automatic flow. They respond well to effort. When you put energy in, the sextile helps things move. It's a green light, but you still have to drive.

□ 90°

Square

Friction · Growth

Two planets 90 degrees apart — at right angles to each other. Squares are the aspect of productive tension. The two planets involved are working at cross-purposes, each one pulling in a direction that doesn't naturally accommodate the other.

This is friction. And friction is how things get made. Squares don't let you coast. They force you to find workarounds, to master the energies involved, to develop a skill or a strength you wouldn't have grown without the resistance. A square in your solar return doesn't mean the year will be hard — it means there's somewhere the year is making you work. That work is the point.

△ 120°

Trine

Ease · Flow

Two planets 120 degrees apart. The trine is the most harmonious aspect — the energies involved flow together effortlessly. They're in compatible signs, operating in aligned elements, and they support each other without friction or effort.

Trines are a gift. They're where the year moves easily, where things open up without a fight, where natural talent or good fortune shows up. The one thing to watch: ease can breed complacency. A trine can feel so comfortable that you don't push into it. When you do, the results tend to be remarkable — because the support is already there.

☍ 180°

Opposition

Tension · Awareness

Two planets directly across the chart from each other — 180 degrees apart, sitting in opposite houses. Oppositions create a push-pull dynamic. Both planets are active, both want their due, and they're pulling in opposite directions. You feel this as a tension between two areas of life that both need your attention.

Oppositions bring things into awareness. What's across from you becomes visible in a way it might not have been. This is often where the most important confrontations of the year happen — with other people, with competing priorities, with the parts of yourself that want different things. The opposition doesn't resolve. You learn to hold both sides. That's the growth.

When a planet is conjunct the Sun: combustion

Conjunctions are a merging of energies — but when a planet is conjunct the Sun, something specific happens. The Sun's light is immense. When another planet is very close to it in the chart, the Sun's rays can overwhelm that planet's individual expression.

This is called combustion — and a planet is considered combust when it sits within roughly 8 degrees of the Sun.

A combust planet doesn't disappear — but its independent voice gets absorbed into the Sun's story. It operates through solar themes rather than on its own terms.

In practice: if Mercury is combust in your solar return, the mind is intensely oriented toward the Sun's house and themes — communication and thinking are funneled into that central preoccupation of the year, rather than operating freely. If Venus is combust, pleasure, beauty, and connection are similarly absorbed into the solar focus.

Combustion isn't always a problem. Sometimes that concentration of energy is exactly what a year calls for. But it's worth knowing which planet is sitting so close to the Sun — because that planet's usual independence is dimmed. The Sun is running the show for both of them.

When the Sun, Moon, or Ascendant ruler conjuncts a malefic

In traditional astrology, planets are categorized as benefics — those that tend to bring ease and blessing — and malefics — those that tend to bring challenge, intensity, and pressure. In modern astrology we use both, and neither is simply "bad." But the distinction matters, especially when it comes to conjunctions.

When your SR Sun, SR Moon, or the ruler of your SR Ascendant forms a conjunction with a malefic planet, it marks the year as one of significant intensity — not in a way to fear, but in a way to prepare for. These are the years that change you. They are highly transformative, full of hard work, drive, and growth. What kind of transformation depends on which malefic is involved.

The Malefics

Mars · Saturn · Pluto

Mars ♂ Urgency, drive, ambition, conflict. A Mars conjunction with the Sun, Moon, or Ascendant ruler signals an intensely active year — things get done, sometimes forcefully. Energy is high. So is the potential for friction, impatience, or outright conflict. The growth is in learning to direct that fire rather than be burned by it.
Saturn ♄ Discipline, hard work, restriction, and lessons earned slowly. A Saturn conjunction is a year of maturity through limitation — things may feel heavy, slow, or demanding. What you build this year is built to last precisely because Saturn made you do it properly. The growth is real and the results are lasting, even when the process is hard.
Pluto ♇ Profound transformation, power dynamics, deep excavation. A Pluto conjunction is one of the most significant placements in any solar return — the year dismantles something in order to rebuild it from the ground up. Old structures, identities, or relationships that can no longer hold the weight of who you're becoming will fall away. Nothing stays the same. The growth is irreversible.

If you have one of these conjunctions in your solar return chart, read it as a year of initiation — not punishment. The pressure is purposeful. The intensity is proportional to what's trying to emerge. You're not in a hard year. You're in a year that's making you into someone your easier years couldn't have.

The Benefics — for contrast

Venus · Jupiter

Venus ♀ Love, beauty, pleasure, and connection. A Venus conjunction with the Sun, Moon, or Ascendant ruler brings a warmth and grace to the year — relationships are nourishing, creativity flows, and the things that bring you joy feel more accessible.
Jupiter ♃ Expansion, abundance, and good fortune. A Jupiter conjunction is often the most welcome placement in a solar return — things open up, opportunities multiply, and the year carries a quality of generosity and growth. The caution: Jupiter can also expand what you don't want, and can encourage excess. More isn't always better.

Reading aspects in practice

You don't need to read every line on the chart. Start with the planets you already know are significant in your solar return — your SR Sun, SR Moon, your Ascendant ruler. Look at what aspects those planets are making.

For each aspect, ask three questions:

Question one

Which planets?

What energies are in relationship? A Sun-Jupiter trine is a very different conversation than a Sun-Saturn square. Know who's talking before you try to hear what they're saying.

Question two

Which houses?

The planets are connecting across the chart — which areas of life are being linked? A Mercury-Venus sextile between the 3rd and 5th houses says something about communication and creativity working together. The houses tell you where the conversation is happening.

Question three

What kind of aspect?

Is this ease or friction? A trine says these two energies are flowing together and amplifying each other. A square says they're in productive tension — one is demanding something of the other. The aspect type tells you the quality of the conversation.

Put those three things together and you have the sentence. The planets are the subject and object. The houses are the setting. The aspect is the verb — how the energies are acting on each other.

With practice, you'll start to read the chart as a web of conversations rather than a collection of individual placements. That's when the story of the year really comes alive.

Look at which planets are doing significant work in your solar return — prominent house placements, strong aspects, rulership of your Ascendant.
